### **Sequencing Strategy: Safe to Risky** (*Cross-Examination*, p. 241, 485)

> "A third distinct strategy of cross-examination sequencing is to provide the cross-examining lawyer with methods of discouraging the witness from fighting back... Certain sequencing techniques work to suppress the will of the witness to contradict."

1. **Chapters I-II** (Safe): Jostes must agree with basic facts ‚Üí Builds control

- Power close capitalizes on momentum

- Third use: Final commitments chapter

### **The Trilogy: Rule of Three** (*Cross-Examination*, p. 689, 703)

> "Trilogies have a cadence, a rhythm, a stirring that makes them more memorable than other forms... From this man's lips, we learned that he lies. From this man's lips, we learned that he deceives. From this man's lips, we learned that he falsifies."

3. "You never gave the insured or assignee an opportunity to address your concerns, did you?"

- Three parallel structures create rhythm
- Memorable for jury during deliberations
- Establishes pattern of professional failure

## **MISSOURI LAW FOUNDATIONS - DETAILED**

### **Vexatious Refusal to Pay (¬ß375.420)**

**Statutory Text:**

> "In any action against an insurer... for refusal to pay a claim, if it appears from the evidence that such insurer has vexatiously refused to pay such claim, the court or jury... may allow the plaintiff... an attorney's fee... and an amount not to exceed the greater of (a) Twice the amount of any actual damages; or (b) Two thousand dollars."

**Elements Proven in This Cross-Examination:**

1. **No Reasonable Cause**
   - **Chapters IV + VII:** No legal research (Chapter IV) + No factual investigation (Chapter VII) = No reasonable cause
   - **Missouri Law:** *DeWitt v. Shelter Mut. Ins. Co.* - Inadequate investigation establishes no reasonable cause

2. **Willful**
   - **Chapter VIII:** Persistence despite notice proves willfulness
   - **Missouri Law:** *Qureshi v. GEICO* - Conduct through trial date relevant to vexatious refusal analysis

3. **Bad Faith**
   - **Chapter VI:** Financial motive ($4.5M vs. $1.46M) proves bad faith
   - **Chapters III + V:** Arbitrary timing + shifting explanations = bad faith
